🇦🇺 Australia
1. Sydney Opera House & Harbour Bridge (Sydney)
The definitive Australian landmark. The dramatic sail-like shells of the Opera house paired with the massive steel arch of the Harbour Bridge create one of the most recognizable harbor views in the world.

2. The Great Barrier Reef (Queensland)
The world's largest coral reef system, visible from space. It stretches over 2,300 kilometers and is a paradise for scuba divers, snorkelers, and marine life enthusiasts.

3. Uluru / Ayers Rock (Northern Territory)
A massive sandstone monolith in the heart of the Northern Territory's arid "Red Centre." It holds deep spiritual significance for Indigenous Australians.

4. Twelve Apostles (Victoria)
A collection of magnificent limestone stacks rising majestically from the Southern Ocean along the famous Great Ocean Road.

5. Blue Mountains (New South Wales)
Known for dramatic scenery, eucalyptus forests, waterfalls, and the famous Three Sisters rock formation ruggedly carved into the landscape.

🇨🇦 Canada
1. Banff National Park & Lake Louise (Alberta)
Canada's oldest national park, famed for its surreal, turquoise-colored glacial lakes, towering peaks of the Canadian Rockies, and abundant wildlife.

2. Niagara Falls (Ontario)
One of the world's most powerful and famous waterfalls, straddling the border between Canada and the USA. The Canadian side offers the iconic Horseshoe Falls view.

3. CN Tower & Toronto Skyline (Ontario)
Defining the Toronto skyline, this 553-meter-high concrete communications and observation tower offers panoramic views of Lake Ontario.

4. Old Quebec City (Quebec)
A UNESCO World Heritage treasure, this historic neighborhood features fortified colonial walls, cobblestone streets, and the striking Château Frontenac.

5. Vancouver Seawall & Stanley Park (British Columbia)
A massive green oasis right in the midst of urban Vancouver, surrounded by the waters of Vancouver Harbour and English Bay.

🇸🇬 Singapore
1. Gardens by the Bay
A futuristic nature park featuring the iconic, plant-covered Supertree Structures, the massive Cloud Forest dome, and the Flower Dome.

2. Marina Bay Sands
The defining luxury resort of Singapore's skyline, famous for its three cascading hotel towers topped by a massive sky park and infinity pool.

3. Sentosa Island & Universal Studios
A dedicated resort island featuring pristine artificial beaches, world-class golf courses, luxury dining, and Singapore's premier theme park.

4. Jewel Changi Airport
An architectural marvel inside the airport, housing the Rain Vortex—the world’s tallest indoor waterfall—surrounded by a terraced forest setting.

5. Chinatown & Sri Mariamman Temple
A vibrant mix of old and new, featuring traditional shophouses, bustling night markets, and the historic, intricately carved Sri Mariamman Hindu temple.

🇻🇳 Vietnam
1. Ha Long Bay
A UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring thousands of towering limestone karsts and isles rising dramatically out of calm, emerald waters.

2. Hoi An Ancient Town
A exceptionally well-preserved example of a Southeast Asian trading port from the 15th to the 19th century, famous for its colorful paper lanterns at night.

3. Sa Pa Terraces
Stunning, cascading green rice terraces carved into the mountainous terrain of northwestern Vietnam, often shrouded in a mystical mist.

4. Cu Chi Tunnels (Ho Chi Minh City)
An immense network of underground connecting tunnels used by the Viet Cong during the Vietnam War, offering a raw look into wartime history.

5. Phong Nha-Ke Bang Caves
Home to Son Doong, the largest cave on the planet. The park features spectacular karst formations and massive, ancient underground river systems.

🇹🇭 Thailand
1. The Grand Palace & Wat Phra Kaew (Bangkok)
A dazzling complex of highly ornate buildings and temples in the heart of Bangkok, serving as the official residence of the Kings of Siam since 1782.

2. Phi Phi Islands (Krabi)
Stunning tropical islands rising from the Andaman Sea, featuring sheer limestone cliffs that tower over turquoise waters and white sandy bays like Maya Bay.

3. Chiang Mai Old City & Wat Phra That Doi Suthep
The cultural heart of Northern Thailand, surrounded by ancient brick walls and boasting golden mountain temples overlooking the mist-covered city.

4. Ayutthaya Historical Park
The atmospheric ruins of the second capital of the Kingdom of Siam, filled with towering reliquary stupas and Buddha statues entwined in tree roots.

5. Railay Beach (Krabi)
Accessible only by traditional longtail boat due to high limestone cliffs cutting off mainland access, it's a world-famous spot for rock climbers and beach lovers.

🇫🇯 Fiji
1. Mamanuca Islands
A collection of 20 volcanic islands known for crystal-clear waters, vibrant coral reefs, and palm-fringed sandy beaches. It’s where Cast Away was filmed.

2. Yasawa Islands
A rugged chain of islands ideal for travelers seeking unspoiled nature, spectacular hiking tracks, and swimming with manta rays.

3. Coral Coast (Viti Levu)
An 80-kilometer stretch of brilliant beaches and bays along the southern coast of Fiji's main island, heavily lined with coral reefs.

4. Taveuni Island (The Garden Island)
A paradise for hikers and eco-tourists, famous for its lush tropical rainforests, hidden waterfalls, and the Bouma National Heritage Park.

5. Beqa Lagoon
Located off the southern coast of Viti Levu, this spot is globally legendary for world-class shark diving and traditional Fijian fire-walking ceremonies.

🇮🇩 Indonesia
1. Uluwatu Temple & Cliffs (Bali)
A spectacular sea temple perched precariously on a steep cliff face edge, dropping roughly 70 meters directly into the roaring Indian Ocean below.

2. Borobudur Temple (Central Java)
The world's largest Buddhist temple, dating back to the 9th century. It features monumental step-pyramid structures decorated with hundreds of Buddha statues.

3. Komodo National Park (Lesser Sunda Islands)
The volcanic islands where the legendary, giant Komodo Dragons roam free. It also features the world-renowned Pink Beach.

4. Mount Bromo (East Java)
An active volcanic peak sitting inside the massive Tengger caldera, surrounded by a surreal "sea of sand" that looks completely otherworldly at sunrise.

5. Raja Ampat Islands (West Papua)
An archipelago comprising over 1,500 small isles, keys, and shoals. It is widely considered the global epicenter of marine biodiversity.

🇿🇦 South Africa
1. Table Mountain & Cape Town
The flat-topped mountain that forms a prominent landmark overlooking the vibrant coastal city of Cape Town and its historic harbors.

2. Kruger National Park
One of Africa's largest game reserves, teeming with an incredible density of wild animals, including the iconic "Big Five" (lions, leopards, rhinos, elephants, and buffalos).

3. The Garden Route
A scenic stretch of the southeastern coast of South Africa, featuring varied vegetation, expansive lagoons, and dramatic coastal cliffs.

4. Blyde River Canyon (Mpumalanga)
One of the largest and greenest canyons on Earth, famous for spectacular viewpoints like the "Three Rondavels" and "God's Window."

5. Boulders Beach Penguin Colony (Simons Town)
A sheltered beach made of massive granite boulders, home to a famous, thriving colony of land-based African Penguins.

🇺🇸 USA
1. The Grand Canyon (Arizona)
A powerful and inspiring landscape, the Grand Canyon overwhelms our senses through its immense size: 446 km long and up to 29 km wide.

2. Statue of Liberty & New York Skyline (New York)
The universal symbol of freedom that welcomes millions of visitors to New York City harbor, backed by Manhattan's skyscrapers.

3. Yellowstone National Park (Wyoming/Montana/Idaho)
The world's first national park, famous for its geothermal wonders like the Old Faithful geyser and the vivid colors of the Grand Prismatic Spring.

4. Golden Gate Bridge (San Francisco, California)
Once called "the bridge that couldn't be built," today it is a world-renowned engineering marvel stretching across the San Francisco Bay.

5. Yosemite National Park (California)
Famed for its giant, ancient sequoia trees, the towering granite cliffs of El Capitan and Half Dome, and plunging waterfalls.

🏴󠁧󠁢󠁥󠁮󠁧󠁿 England
1. Big Ben & The Palace of Westminster (London)
The iconic clock tower and Gothic revival palace sitting on the banks of the River Thames, serving as the heart of British political power.

2. Stonehenge (Wiltshire)
One of the wonders of the world and the best-known prehistoric monument in Europe, erected over 4,500 years ago.

3. The Roman Baths (Bath)
A beautifully preserved historic site in the heart of the city of Bath, showcasing the ancient public bathing complexes built above natural hot springs.

4. The Lake District (Cumbria)
A stunning mountainous region in northwest England, famous for its glacial ribbon lakes, rugged fell mountains, and literary associations.

5. Tower Bridge (London)
A magnificent combination of bascule and suspension bridge architecture built in the late 19th century, iconic for its twin towers.

🇮🇪 Ireland
1. Cliffs of Moher (County Clare)
Stunning, sheer sea cliffs that run for about 14 kilometers along the wild Atlantic coast, plunging over 200 meters into the ocean.

2. The Ring of Kerry
A 179-kilometer-long scenic drive circling the Iveragh Peninsula, taking visitors through rolling green hills, jagged coastlines, and traditional villages.

3. Trinity College Long Room (Dublin)
The 18th-century main chamber of the Old Library, housing 200,000 of the library’s oldest books, including the legendary Book of Kells.

4. Giant's Causeway (Northern Ireland Border area / Shared region interest)
An area of about 40,000 interlocking basalt columns, the result of an ancient volcanic fissure eruption (technically located in Northern Ireland, a major island itinerary spot).

5. Killarney National Park
The first national park in Ireland, containing an extensive ecosystem of native oak woods, majestic red deer herds, and the Lakes of Killarney.

🏴󠁧󠁢󠁳󠁣󠁴󠁿 Scotland
1. Edinburgh Castle
A historic fortress which dominates the skyline of the city of Edinburgh from its position on the volcanic Castle Rock.

2. Isle of Skye
Famous for its rugged, dramatic landscapes, picturesque fishing villages, and medieval castles. Features the stunning geological formations of the Quiraing.

3. Loch Ness & Urquhart Castle
A large, deep, freshwater loch in the Scottish Highlands made famous worldwide by sightings of the mythical Loch Ness Monster, featuring historic castle ruins on its bank.

4. Glenfinnan Viaduct
A spectacular, curved railway viaduct deep in the highlands, instantly recognizable to global audiences as the bridge crossed by the Hogwarts Express.

5. Glencoe Valley
A volcanic glen of haunting beauty and stark, massive mountain peaks. It holds a deep historical weight as the site of the infamous 1692 Clan MacDonald massacre.

🇳🇿 New Zealand
1. Milford Sound (Fiordland National Park)
Famed by Rudyard Kipling as the eighth wonder of the world, this breathtaking fiord features sheer cliffs, dark glacial waters, and cascading waterfalls.

2. Hobbiton Movie Set (Matamata)
The actual rolling green hills and permanent hobbit hole sets constructed in the Waikato region for The Lord of the Rings and The Hobbit film trilogies.

3. Lake Tekapo & Mount Cook
A stunning alpine lake with intensely milky-turquoise water, backed by New Zealand's highest peak and situated inside an international Dark Sky Reserve.

4. Wai-O-Tapu Thermal Wonderland (Rotorua)
An active geothermal area showcasing spectacular colorful hot springs, boiling mud pools, and the unique, neon-green "Devil's Bath."

5. Queenstown & Lake Wakatipu
The undisputed "Adventure Capital of the World," framed by the dramatic Remarkables mountain range and loved for skiing, bungee jumping, and jet boating.
